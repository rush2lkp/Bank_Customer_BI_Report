# Customer Banking Behavior and KPI Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer demographics, financial behavior, and banking KPIs to generate actionable insights for business decision-making.

The goal is to help the bank:
- Understand customer behavior patterns  
- Identify high-value customer segments  
- Track financial performance  
- Improve marketing and customer engagement strategies  

---

## Objectives

### Customer Behavior Insights
- Analyze customer behavior based on:
  - Marital Status  
  - Age Group  
  - Job Classification  
- Identify high-value customer segments for targeted campaigns  

---

### KPI Dashboard Development
Develop interactive dashboards to track:
- Average Bank Balance  
- Total Deposits  
- Customer Growth Trends  

---

### Financial Profiling and Segmentation
- Segment customers into:
  - High-balance vs Low-balance  
  - Income or Job-based segments  
- Provide personalized banking product recommendations  

---

### Geographical Market Analysis
- Analyze customer distribution across cities or regions  
- Identify high-performing regions  
- Recommend strategies to replicate success in other areas  

---

### Gender-Based Insights
- Compare financial behavior between male and female customers  
- Identify gaps in engagement  
- Suggest strategies to attract and retain female customers

## Problem Statements

### Customer Demographics and Behavior
How do demographic factors such as:
- Marital status  
- Age group  
- Job classification  
- Gender  

influence customer banking behavior and financial trends?

---Based on my observation for the above demographic factors.
1)**Married Customers** 
-Contribute the highest total balances
-Show more stable financial behavior
-Lower loan default rates compared to singles
2)**Single customers**
-Lower overall balances
-Higher loan default rates
-More variability in financial activity
**Business Interpretation**
Married customers = high-value, low-risk segment
Single customers = growth opportunity but higher risk
3)**26–50 age group**
-Highest balances
-Most financially active
-Core revenue-generating segment
4)**18–25**
-Low balances
-Early-stage customers
-Potential for long-term growth
5)**50+ age group**
-Moderate balances
-More conservative financial behavior
**Business Interpretation**
26–50 = Primary target segment
Younger group = Future high-value customers
6)**White Collar jobs**
-Higher average balances
-Lower default rates
7)**Blue Collar jobs**
-Lower balances
-Higher loan default rates

**Business Interpretation**
-Job classification strongly predicts:
-Customer value
-Credit risk

8)**Male customers**
-Slightly higher total balances
-More representation in dataset
9)**Female customers**
-Comparable average balances
-Slightly higher default rate in some segments
**Business Interpretation**
Gender differences are less significant than age & job
Should be used as a secondary segmentation factor

**High-Value Customer Segment**
Married
Age: 26–50
Stable/Professional Jobs
Lower default risk
**High-Risk Segment**
Single
Younger or unstable job roles
Higher loan default rates


**Demographic factors significantly influence customer banking behavior.
Marital status and age are the strongest indicators of financial stability, while job classification is the key predictor of customer value and risk. Gender plays a minor role compared to these factors**

Understanding these patterns enables banks to:

-Target high-value customers effectively
-Minimize credit risk
-Design personalized financial products**
